Fasting, Medical Responsibility, and Safety Terms

Consult a qualified healthcare professional before fasting

Before starting any fast, especially a fast longer than a typical overnight fast, you should consult a qualified healthcare professional who understands your medical history, medications, age, weight, pregnancy or breastfeeding status, diet, activity level, and health conditions. This is especially important for prolonged fasts, repeated fasts, water-only fasts, or fasts longer than 24 hours.

You are responsible for your fasting decisions

You choose whether to start, continue, pause, end, resume, or repeat a fast. You choose what to drink, whether to use electrolytes, how active to be, when to sleep, when to stop, how to refeed, and when to seek medical care. Beyond may display information, reminders, checklists, stop signals, or scores, but those displays do not mean Beyond has verified that fasting is safe for you.

People who should avoid fasting or seek medical guidance first

Do not use Beyond to decide whether fasting is appropriate if you are under 18, pregnant, breastfeeding, underweight, malnourished, recovering from illness or surgery, highly active in a way that requires reliable fueling, or if you have a history of disordered eating, diabetes, hypoglycemia, kidney disease, heart disease, liver disease, gout, gallbladder disease, fainting, seizures, electrolyte disorders, blood pressure problems, or any condition affected by food, fluid, minerals, or medication timing.

If you take insulin, glucose-lowering medication, blood pressure medication, diuretics, psychiatric medication, seizure medication, blood thinners, heart medication, kidney medication, or any medication that may be affected by food intake, hydration, electrolytes, caffeine, or fasting, speak with a healthcare professional before fasting or changing timing.

Stop signals and urgent symptoms

Stop fasting and seek medical attention if you experience fainting, near-fainting, chest pain, confusion, severe weakness, seizure, shortness of breath, rapid or irregular heartbeat, severe or worsening headache, persistent vomiting, severe dizziness, severe dehydration signs, inability to keep fluids down, new neurological symptoms, or any symptom that feels serious, unusual, worsening, or unsafe.

Hydration, electrolytes, caffeine, and allowed drinks

Beyond may show general informational content about water, unsweetened drinks, black coffee, tea, minerals, electrolytes, or refeed planning. This content is not a prescription, nutrition plan, electrolyte protocol, or medical instruction. You are responsible for confirming with a healthcare professional what is appropriate for your body, medications, health status, and fast duration.

Refeeding and ending a fast

Ending a long fast can carry risk if food is restarted too quickly or without an appropriate plan. Beyond may display refeed-planning information, but it does not supervise refeeding, diagnose refeeding risk, prescribe foods, or calculate individual medical needs. For prolonged fasts, repeated fasts, undernutrition, rapid weight loss, or any medical condition, consult a qualified healthcare professional before ending or restarting food.

Information may be incomplete or inaccurate

Scores, charts, check-ins, trend lines, achievements, progress estimates, water progress, steps, sleep, weight changes, photo comparisons, and recommended next actions depend on the information available in the app. They may be wrong, incomplete, delayed, duplicated, stale, or misleading if entries are missing, inaccurate, imported incorrectly, manually entered incorrectly, or not updated.
