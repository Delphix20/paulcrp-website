Privacy Policy and Terms

Last updated: May 18, 2026

This document explains how Viento handles information, how weather data is provided, and the terms that apply when you use the app.

Viento is a minimal weather app designed to show current weather, hourly forecasts, 10-day forecasts, wind, light, air, pressure, saved locations, app settings, and severe weather alerts where available. By using Viento, you agree to the practices described below.

Local-first design: Viento stores your app settings and saved locations on your device using Apple system storage. Viento does not currently operate a custom backend server for your weather history, saved places, or settings.
Weather notice: Weather data, forecasts, severe weather alerts, sunrise and sunset times, pressure, precipitation, humidity, visibility, wind, and related values are informational only. Weather can change quickly, and Viento should not be used as your only source for safety-critical decisions.

What Viento Collects and Stores

Location data

Viento can request access to your device location so it can show weather for your current location. Location permission is controlled by iOS. If you allow location access, Viento may use your current device location to request weather data.

  • Your current location is used to display current weather and forecasts for where you are
  • You can deny or revoke location permission through iPhone Settings
  • If location access is unavailable, some current-location features may not work until you choose or add another location

Saved locations

Viento lets you search for, save, select, and remove weather locations. Saved location data may include a place name, subtitle or region text, latitude, longitude, and the selected location identifier.

  • Saved locations are stored locally on your device
  • Current Location is treated separately from manually saved places
  • Removing the app from your device may delete locally stored saved locations, subject to iOS behavior

Settings and preferences

Viento stores app preferences needed to provide your selected experience, including:

  • Temperature unit, such as Celsius or Fahrenheit
  • Wind unit, such as km/h, m/s, mph, or knots
  • Precipitation unit, such as millimeters or inches
  • Distance unit, such as kilometers or miles
  • Pressure unit, such as hPa, mb, or inHg
  • Severe weather notification preference
  • Whether the first-launch onboarding has been completed

Weather data

Viento uses Apple WeatherKit to request weather data for the selected location. This may include current conditions, temperature, hourly forecast, daily forecast, wind, gusts, wind direction, sunrise, sunset, moon phase, precipitation chance, precipitation amount, humidity, visibility, pressure, pressure trend, and severe weather alerts where available.

Weather data is provided by Apple WeatherKit and related Apple services. Viento does not create the underlying weather forecast data and does not control whether severe weather alerts are available for every location.

Notifications

If you enable Severe alerts in Viento Settings and grant notification permission, Viento may schedule a local iOS notification when a severe weather alert is issued for the selected location and available through WeatherKit.

  • Notification permission is controlled by iOS
  • You can disable notifications inside Viento or through iPhone Settings
  • Notification sound behavior is controlled by iOS notification settings, Focus modes, device volume, and Apple system behavior
  • Viento does not guarantee delivery of every notification
  • Viento does not currently operate a server that continuously monitors weather alerts while the app is closed

Usage and technical data

Viento does not currently operate a custom analytics backend for your saved locations, selected location, or app settings. Limited technical information may still be processed by Apple or system services, such as:

  • App version and build information
  • Device type and operating system version
  • Crash logs and performance diagnostics if you have enabled sharing diagnostics with Apple
  • Notification authorization status managed by iOS
  • Purchase or entitlement status if Viento offers paid features, subscriptions, or in-app purchases in the future

How Viento Uses Your Data

Viento may use stored or permitted data to:

  • Show current weather for your current or selected location
  • Display hourly, daily, wind, light, air, and pressure information
  • Search for and save locations you choose
  • Apply your preferred units and notification settings
  • Show in-app severe weather indicators when alerts are available
  • Schedule local notifications if you enable severe weather notifications
  • Remember that onboarding has been completed
  • Maintain app reliability, stability, and support diagnostics

Storage and Sync

Viento stores core app preferences and saved locations locally on your device using Apple system storage. Viento does not currently provide custom account sync or a custom cloud backend for saved locations or settings.

  • Saved locations and settings are stored on your device
  • Weather requests are made for the selected location when needed
  • Deleting the app may remove local app data from your device, subject to iOS behavior
  • If a future version adds iCloud sync, subscriptions, widgets, or other cloud-connected features, this policy may be updated

Third-Party and Apple Services

Viento relies on Apple-provided systems and services needed to run the app, such as:

  • Core Location for current location permission and location access
  • MapKit or Apple location services for location search and geocoding features
  • WeatherKit for weather conditions, forecasts, and severe weather alerts
  • UserNotifications for local severe weather notifications if enabled
  • App Store and StoreKit if paid features, subscriptions, or in-app purchases are offered in the future
  • Apple diagnostics and crash-reporting systems if you have enabled sharing diagnostics with Apple
