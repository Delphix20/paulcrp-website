Local-first design: The app is designed to load from data stored on your iPhone first. If iCloud sync is enabled, selected app data can sync through Apple's private iCloud services associated with your Apple account.

What My Work Shift Planner Collects and Stores

Data you enter or create

My Work Shift Planner may store information you provide or generate inside the app, including:

  • Your first name, if you choose to enter one
  • Your role or job title, if you choose to enter one
  • Work profile information, including profile name, workplace name, time zone, shift length, and default start times
  • Shift entries, including date, shift type, start time, end time, duration, location or area notes, notes, tags, bonuses, and associated work profile
  • Pay settings, including hourly rate, currency, overtime rules, unpaid break settings, and related estimate settings
  • Rotation preset choices and applied rotation history needed for undo or summaries
  • Reminder settings, including whether reminders are enabled and the selected lead time
  • iCloud sync preference, biometric lock preference, time display preference, and other app settings
  • Generated CSV or PDF export files before you share or save them

Notifications and reminders

If you enable shift reminders and grant notification permission, My Work Shift Planner schedules local iOS notifications before upcoming shifts. These reminders are generated on your device using the shift data stored in the app.

  • Reminder permission is controlled by iOS
  • You can disable reminders inside the app or through iPhone Settings
  • The app does not guarantee delivery of every notification because delivery can depend on iOS settings, Focus modes, notification permissions, device state, and Apple system behavior

Biometric lock

If you enable the app lock feature, My Work Shift Planner may use Apple's local Face ID, Touch ID, or device passcode authentication system to protect access to the app. The app does not receive or store your biometric data.

Usage and technical data

We do not operate a custom analytics backend for your work schedule data or work profile data. Limited technical information may still be processed by Apple or system services, such as:

  • App version and build information
  • Device type and operating system version
  • Crash logs and performance diagnostics if you have enabled sharing diagnostics with Apple
  • Subscription and entitlement status needed to determine whether app access is active, expired, canceled, or restored

How My Work Shift Planner Uses Your Data

My Work Shift Planner may use stored or permitted data to:

  • Create, display, and manage your shift calendar
  • Support multiple work profiles and workplaces
  • Apply rotation presets and show rotation summaries
  • Calculate estimated hours, overtime, gross pay, weekly summaries, monthly summaries, and yearly summaries
  • Schedule local reminders before upcoming shifts
  • Generate CSV and PDF reports
  • Restore or sync your app experience through iCloud if enabled
  • Operate subscriptions, restore purchases, and enforce access
  • Maintain app reliability, stability, and support diagnostics

Storage, Sync, and iCloud

My Work Shift Planner stores core app data locally on your device. If iCloud sync is enabled, the app may use Apple's CloudKit or iCloud systems to sync selected app data and settings across devices associated with your Apple account.

  • Shift entries, work profiles, pay settings, reminder settings, and app preferences may sync through Apple's private iCloud services
  • iCloud sync is tied to your Apple account and compatible devices
  • You can disable iCloud sync in the app settings
  • We do not operate a custom backend server for your work calendar or work profile history at this time unless explicitly stated otherwise inside the app

Exports and Sharing

My Work Shift Planner can generate CSV and PDF reports from your shift, profile, pay, and summary data. Export files are created so you can review, save, or share them using iOS sharing features.

  • Exported files may contain workplace names, shift dates, shift types, times, hours, pay estimates, location or area details, notes, and summary totals
  • After you share, save, email, upload, or send an exported file, that file may be handled by the apps or services you choose
  • You are responsible for deciding where exported files are sent and whether they contain information you consider private or sensitive
